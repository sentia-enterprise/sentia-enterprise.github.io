Built for operational uncertainty, not generic digital transformation.

Many wineries already have skilled teams and established routines. WineOps focuses on the gaps where paper, memory, Excel, or disconnected systems make the next decision slower than it should be.

Late reaction

Fermentation deviations or room events are noticed, but the response path is not always visible across shifts.

Asset uncertainty

Barrels, lots, movements, and exceptions can become hard to reconstruct when the team needs a clear history.

Evidence gap

Teams may know what happened, but lack a simple evidence pack for review, audit support, or commercial discussion.

A modular portfolio for practical winery pilots.

Start with the module that matches one current pain. Add expansion modules only when adoption, risk, or commercial priority makes the next step credible.

Core

FermentWatch

Early alerts, tank history, event closure, and response-time evidence for fermentation control.

Core

BarrelTrack

Traceability for barrel locations, movements, and operating events across defined winery zones.

Decision layer

BarrelIQ

Exception patterns, decision notes, and escalation logic for barrel-related decisions.

Expansion

CellarClimate

Room or zone climate visibility when conditions affect quality routines or internal audit evidence.

Expansion

WineTransit

Shipment-condition evidence for premium or export lots where dispatch risk needs better records.

90-day pilot

Evidence before rollout.

The pilot is deliberately narrow: one operational pain, one scope, one baseline, and one go / no-go recommendation at the end.

3

Operating routine

Final evidence pack

Review adoption, unresolved exceptions, response routines, and the case for continuing or stopping.
